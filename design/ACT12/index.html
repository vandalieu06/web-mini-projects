// The Story

Born from Engineers, Built for Artists

In 1992, a team at IBM's Yamato Laboratory asked a simple question: "What if a laptop was designed by the people who actually use it?" The result was ThinkPad—a name that would become synonymous with reliability.

Every X1 Carbon contains over 500 components, each precision-engineered to work in harmony. The iconic red TrackPoint isn't just a pointing device—it's a statement that the best tools are built by those who understand the craft.

Today, from Linux kernel developers to mission control centers, ThinkPad powers the systems that matter. This is not just a laptop. This is a legacy of engineering excellence.
